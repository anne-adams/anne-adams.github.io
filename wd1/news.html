Online news consumption has surpassed television – but broadcasters are still the most widely trusted

Stephen Cushion discusses the shift in the UK's news consumption habits, where online sources have overtaken television
for the first time. Despite this change, traditional broadcasters like the BBC continue to be the most trusted news
providers, especially during significant events such as elections and public health crises.
The article highlights that while younger audiences increasingly access news via social media, much of the content
originates from established media outlets, indicating that trust in traditional journalism remains strong even in the
digital age.
